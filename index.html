HOW IT ALL STARTED – BY LEWIS THE CEO

From a young age, I found myself deeply drawn to creativity and crafting. In school, while many of my peers focused on competitive drawing—especially cars—I developed a strong desire not only to participate, but to improve, excel, and master skill through dedication. That early environment sparked curiosity, discipline, and ambition in me.

My turning point came when I met Elvis, who gathered children and taught us painting fundamentals like color blending, brush control, and emotional expression. That experience opened my eyes to art as a language, not just a skill.

I later enrolled in structured pencil training, but soon realized I wanted more than technique—I wanted emotion, color, and meaning. I began self-teaching through observation, videos, and constant practice.

A breakthrough came when a visitor saw my work and invited me to the African Kidpreneur Summit, where I witnessed young artists building real opportunities from creativity. There I presented my ideas and portfolio, gaining encouragement that changed my direction.

After the summit, I received support that allowed me to buy materials and fully begin my journey. That moment marked the birth of what is now the Art Is Life Foundation.

Today, I create not just to paint—but to build a future where art becomes opportunity, identity, and life itself.
