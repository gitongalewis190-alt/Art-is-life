How It All Started — My Untold Story

My Identity — CEO of Art Is Life

This is who I am. Art Is Life Foundation is not just a brand — it is my identity, my voice, and my purpose. As its CEO and founder, every decision I make, every stroke I paint, carries the weight of who I am and who I am becoming. This is my official profile and artistic presence.

The Beginning — Early Artworks & Foundation

From a young age, I was drawn to creativity in a way I could not fully explain. While others around me focused on structured competition in drawing — especially cars and technical sketches — I found myself wanting something deeper. I didn't just want to draw; I wanted to express feeling, emotion, and meaning through every line. These early works represent the raw, honest foundation of my creative journey.

The Turning Point — Kitmanua Summit & Elvis

A defining moment came at the Kitmanua Summit, where Elvis influenced and guided my artistic growth in ways I never expected. He introduced us to the foundations of painting — how to blend colors, control the brush, and most importantly, how to express emotion through art. That moment changed everything: my technique, my vision, my confidence. A mentor who believed in the art before I fully did.

"That moment changed how I saw creativity — it was no longer just a skill, but a language."

The Vision — What I Aim to Build & Become

I see a future where my art reaches the world — galleries, collections, conversations across continents. Art Is Life Foundation is my dream institution: one that uplifts artists across Kenya, creates pathways for young talent, and proves that African art belongs in every corner of the world. This is not a dream. It is a plan. Every piece I create moves me closer.

Current Work — Present Skill, Progress & Maturity

This is where I stand today. The progress is real. The skill is sharper. The artistry is more mature. Art Is Life is no longer a beginning — it is a movement in motion. Each piece now carries not just emotion, but mastery. Today, my work is not just about painting images — it is about telling human stories that words cannot fully capture.

"Art for me is not just life — it is evidence of life itself."
